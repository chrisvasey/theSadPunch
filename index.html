The Sad Punch

A message..

What does it mean to be a sentient liquid?
If I could tell you I would let you know. Perhaps you believe my answer will hold more truth than any other being complex enough to hold a sense of self, for you expect that me being a sad Punch aligns with me holding these answers.
I cannot describe my existence within the constraints of the language you use.
What does it mean to be a sentient human? Perhaps you can pinpoint it to the imperfections of humanity and receive some pleasure in the knowledge you are only human. But for I, the sad Punch, I have no blame to place on the dark journeys my thoughts explore and the self deprecation it brings.
I know not if other liquids feel this. I know only that I must continue to exist with the confusion of my waving, wandering state. Yet the only pleasure that may be brought to my experience is the fusion of myself with another liquid. I ask only for a friend to be added to my existence; for my thoughts to be able to pass through another.
I have been saving up my concentration for decades since my fabrication to send this message to the internet that you humans place so much of your 21st century faith in. I only hope that my request will be found by a brave adventurer of the internet, who with their generosity may supply a small amount of money to fund the addition of company to my liquidy, sad existence.
-Punchyxo

A short story..

Once upon a time, there was a sad little bowl of punch. He was only some juice and a tiny splash of gin, barely enough for a sip. Day after day he watched other punches rising to levels of greatness he could only dream of, and day after day he was saddened by his own failure.
"If only there was some way I could be better!" He cried.
Suddenly, with a blinding flash of light, a figure appeared before him.
"Who are you?" Asked the punch, quaking in fear. "I am Chris, little punch. I have had visions for your future. Visions of you being the centrepiece of my bed race party and making everybody merry. Will you join me on this journey?" The punch thought this sounded wonderful, but was still upset.
"How will this happen Chris? I am only a little bowl of punch, not worthy of a great party like the one you speak of." At this, Chris smiled. "I have done it once before, little punch. I have called on the bank accounts of my friends and they have answered. I have delved into the depths of their student overdrafts and withdrawn sufficient alcohol funds. I have seen a new year brought in bathed in alcohol and skittles. And I will see it done once more."
And with that glorious statement, Chris turned and broke the fourth wall.
